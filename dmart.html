Dmart...discount or premium


Industry Analysis

The Indian retail space is a growing market, contributing over 10% to the country's gross domestic product (GDP) and employing approximately 8% of the workforce. This sector is not limited to major cities and metros; it is also experiencing growth in small cities. Categories such as apparel, cosmetics, footwear, watches, beverages, food, and jewelry are witnessing advancements and growth. The key factors driving this massive growth can be summarized as follows:

  • Change in Taste and Preferences: There is an increasing demand for quality products and a preference for branded goods.
  • Changing Demographics: Urbanization and a growing middle class segment are contributing to the sector's growth.
  • Economy and Income: Rising purchasing power, healthy economic growth, and increasing disposable income are driving consumer spending.
  • Technological Advancements: Direct-to-customer shipments, the introduction of Unified Payments Interface (UPI), improved logistic management, and user-friendly applications supporting streamlined shopping experiences have enhanced the sector's growth.
    According to a recent analysis by the Boston Consulting Group (BCG), the retail sector in India is projected to reach a value of US$2 trillion by 2032.


    Company Analysis

    Applying Porter's Five Forces model:

  • Threat of Substitutes: Dmart faces minimal threats from competitors like Reliance Retail (Smart Bazaar) and TATA-backed Star Bazaar, as they are still in the introductory stage. Dmart has a higher store count, better customer reach, and a presence in multiple states.
  • Threat of New Entrants: The retail super mart segment requires significant capital expenditure and time to gain popularity among consumers. While there may be local retail super marts in cities, competing against a giant like Dmart, with its greater resources and customer reach, is not an easy task. However, foreign players like Walmart, with extensive capital availability and management, pose a potential threat in the future.
  • Bargaining Power of Customers: Customers visit Dmart stores for discounts and savings. The absence of customer bargaining power works in Dmart's favor. The tagline "Daily Discounts Daily Savings" is effectively fulfilled, and there are no notable issues regarding customer bargaining power.
  • Bargaining Power of Suppliers: Dmart operates on a low cost of goods sold by directly acquiring products from manufacturers in bulk, thereby enjoying significant discounts. Its business model revolves around selling at low prices and buying at even lower prices. Therefore, there are no visible threats from suppliers.
  • Competition in the Industry: Local "kirana stores" and small dairy shops do not pose significant competition due to their size, area, and operating costs. The major players in the market, such as Reliance's Smart Bazaar and TATA Group's Star Bazaar, present real competition. However, Dmart holds a strong market position and benefits from its extensive store network, allowing it to maintain a significant market share. Online vendors like Amazon Fresh, Jio Mart, Flipkart Groceries, Grofers, Swiggy Instamart, and Zomato are also entering the race, although online grocery selling is a relatively new concept in India, it can be tough competition.


    SWOT Analysis:

  • Strengths: Existing customer base and high brand recall value. Competitive edge over peers in terms of the number of stores and coverage areas.
  • Weaknesses: Low net profit margin (averaged at 4.45%). High cost of goods sold (averaged at 89.93%), which may pose a threat when sales are insufficient.
  • Opportunities: Possibility of entering online retailing at a comparatively lower investment in areas where stores are located due to ease of delivery operations.
  • Threats: Intense competition in the retail space. Growing trend of online purchasing, while Dmart is primarily focused on physical stores.

    Financial Analysis:

  • Consistent sales growth above 25%, except in 2021 (-2.92%).
  • Consistency in gross profit margin, averaging around 9.7% with the highest margin achieved in FY2023 at 13.33%.
  • Reduction in the interest expenses-to-sales ratio, indicating debt repayment by the company to improve return on equity (ROE).
  • Rising earnings before taxes (EBT) margins, mainly driven by sales contribution.
  • Positive earnings per share (EPS) growth for all years except FY2021, with the highest growth rate observed in FY2018 at 68.4%.
  • The company has not historically paid dividends, indicating a commitment to retain 100% of the profits for future growth.
  • Borrowings were repaid between FY2018 and FY2021, but there has been an 80% increase in borrowings thereafter.
  • No significant changes in the share capital.
  • A significant amount has been transferred to reserves, although the reasons are unknown due to the unavailability of the annual report for FY23.
  • The company has made significant investments, the reasons for which are also unknown due to the unavailability of the annual report for FY23.
  • Cash flow from operations has experienced a two-fold growth, with revenue from operations increasing by 38%.
